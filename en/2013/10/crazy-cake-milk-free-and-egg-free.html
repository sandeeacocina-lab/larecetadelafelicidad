Depression cake

,

Wacky cake

or

Crazy cake

is a type of cake that was commonly made during the Great Depression (others say during WW II). The ingredients include no milk, butter or eggs, because they were then either expensive or hard to get at the time. The cake is amazingly rich, moist and flavorful, although it contains no butter or eggs... and it can be mixed in the baking pan, without mixer, no bowls, super easy... what's not to like?

Crazy Cake

Preparation time: 10 min

Cooking time: 20 min

Yield: 8x11 inches baking pan (12 people)

Ingredients

  • Cake
  • 1 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 3 tbs unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 5 tbs vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • Dairy free chocolate frosting
  • 200 ml water (0,8 cup)
  • 1 tbs cornstarch (20 g)
  • 250 g dark chocolate (dairy free), coarsely chopped (9 oz)

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350º F (180º C).Put flour, sugar, salt, baking soda, and cocoa together into a 8 x 11 inch ungreased baking pan.
  2. Make three wells. Pour oil into one well, vinegar into second, and vanilla into third well.
  3. Pour cold water over all, and stir well with fork.
  4. Bake at 350°F. oven for 25 to 30 minutes, or until it springs back when touched lightly. Let cool
  5. To prepare the chocolate frosting, in a small bowl, mix water and cornstarch, until disolved
  6. In a medium saucepan, heat over low fire. Add chopped chocolate, and stir constantly until smooth
  7. Pour over cold chocolate cake, and chill until chocolate frosting is set
  8. Keep in the fridge (take out 15-30 minutes before serving) Use a stencil and some powdered sugar to decorate just before serving
NOTES:
